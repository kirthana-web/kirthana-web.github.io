Open-Makerspace

Academic, 2022.

Read More

In short,

Open-Makerspace is service envisioned to improve urban material-culture interactions in Indian cities to rethink how public spaces like makerspaces can influence social, cultural, and economic aspects of urban life.

Context

Traditionally, makerspaces have been limited to academic and learning environments. They offer support in an educational setup, however, makerspaces can affect the social, imaginative and economical fabric of a society. Having had access to formal making much later during my undergraduate courses, I've started to become cognizant of the role making could play in a society.

Multiple metropolitan cities in India are constantly evolving in terms of developing hotspots for entertainment and discussions. Libraries served the purpose earlier but did so with a number of restrictions implicitly.

Approach

Working throughout this project encompassed several design activities, what does it take for a speculative vision to manifest?
Three semi-structured interviews were conducted on the premises of 3-D printers as social tools, motivations for collaboration, crafts ecosystem and operationalising ‘makerspace’ like entities alongside other conversations.The inquiry tried to answer three different questions.

Will a makerspace enable ‘making’?

What would enable the validity of ‘making’ as a means of production for genuine self-usage?

How can a makerspace help shape public vision of the city?

As imagined
